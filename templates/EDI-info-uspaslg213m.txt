Abstract:
    PA SGL213 Marsh is a terrestrial acoustic localization array located in Pennsylvania's State Game Lands 213 (SGL213) with the purpose of investigating the fine-scale habitat associations of wetland-obligate breeding birds. This dataset contains spatially localized events of Swamp sparrow (Melospiza georgiana) vocalizations. The marsh is situated along a lake outlet. Standing water never exceeded 1.5 meters, but could vary from 10cm to 1.5m within the array. Dominant vegetation within the marsh consisted of cattail (Typha latifolia), Swamp loosestrife (Decodon verticillatus), Red maple (Acer rubrum) palustrine woodlands, and emergent-forb marsh composed of pickerelweed (Pontederia cordata), arrow-arum (Peltandra virginica), and arrowhead (Sagittaria latifolia). The array consists of 36 AudioMoth 1.2.0 units with attached GPS receivers attached to trees and PVC poles in a 200x200m grid, with recorders spaced 40 meters apart in 6 parallel rows. Only 32 AudioMoths successfully recorded. AudioMoths recorded from May 8th, 2025 to May 30th, 2025. The daily recording schedule consisted of 10 15-minute recording periods starting in the late evening (7pm E.S.T.) and ending in the early morning (7am E.S.T.), totaling 2.5 hours of recording per day. Species classification was achieved by running HawkEars v1.0 (Huus et al. 2025) on synchronized audio, and sounds were localized using the localization module within the OpenSoundscape Python package (Lapp et al. 2023, Freeland-Haynes et al. 2026). 

Methods:
    Collection: The array consists of 36 AudioMoth 1.2.0 units with attached GPS receivers attached to trees and PVC poles in a 200x200m grid, with recorders spaced 40 meters apart in 6 parallel rows. Only 32 AudioMoths successfully recorded. AudioMoths recorded from May 8th, 2025 to May 30th, 2025. The daily recording schedule consisted of 10 15-minute recording periods starting in the late evening (7pm E.S.T.) and ending in the early morning (7am E.S.T.), totaling 2.5 hours of recording per day. Species classification was achieved by running HawkEars v1.0 (Huus et al. 2025) on synchronized audio, and sounds were localized using the localization module within the OpenSoundscape Python package (Lapp et al. 2023, Freeland-Haynes et al. 2026). 
    
    Validation: Error filtering at the localization step consisted of applying a threshold of -1 to HawkEars output, requiring a minimum of four recorders for each candidate event, using a maximum receiver distance of 200 meters, filtering out any events with residual mean square error values (RMS) > 5, and selecting within the suites of candidate events for each three-second time window by selecting the event with the lowest RMS. 5861 localized events were generated through this process. Each event was reviewed by generating the minimum composite spectrogram (we are going to need to explain this process somewhere) and reviewing each resulting spectrogram in Dipper v0.13.0. Events were visually and auditorially inspected and removed if (1) The dominant sound in the minimum composite spectrogram was not a Swamp Sparrow, even if a Swamp Sparrow was faintly present in the spectrogram (2) If Swamp Sparrow was the dominant sound in the spectrogram, but the composite spectrograms looked to be unaligned, which presents as a blurry or “smudged” spectrogram (3) If no dominant sound was clear in the spectrogram. After this review 5401 localized events remained.
